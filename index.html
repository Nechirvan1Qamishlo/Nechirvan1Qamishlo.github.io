About

I am a PhD candidate in Business Economics and Management at the István Széchenyi Economics and Management Doctoral School, University of Sopron — supported by the Stipendium Hungaricum scholarship.

My research centres on local NGOs operating in crisis-affected areas — specifically how organisations born from necessity transform into strategically aware, sustainably performing actors. I draw on ten years of practitioner experience with international and local organisations across Northeast Syria, including roles at Norwegian People's Aid and CARE International.

Beyond my dissertation, I have published on knowledge management in NGOs, cross-cultural volunteering, and strategic entrepreneurship. I teach EU-funded project management, strategic management, and entrepreneurship as an assistant lecturer.

Research

PhD Dissertation

"From Sudden Birth to Strategic Roles: The Evolution of Strategic Positioning and Sustainable Performance of LNGOs Operating in Crisis-Affected Areas — Evidence from Syria"

This dissertation examines how local non-governmental organisations (LNGOs) that emerged organically from the Syrian crisis have evolved their strategic management capacities over time. It investigates how crisis-born organisations build legitimacy, develop adaptive capabilities, and achieve sustainable performance within fragile institutional environments.

◈

Strategic Positioning

How LNGOs transition from reactive crisis response to deliberate, mission-aligned strategy in contested environments.

◈

Sustainable Performance

Resource configurations, governance mechanisms, and stakeholder relations that enable NPO sustainability under institutional pressure.

◈

Knowledge Management

Inter-organisational learning, knowledge transfer, and capability building in local vs. international NGO contexts.

◈

Crisis Contexts

Institutional fragility, legal ambiguity, and political volatility as antecedent conditions shaping organisational decisions.

Latest Working Paper

Sustainable Performance in Nonprofit Organizations: A Systematic Literature Review Using the TCCM Framework

A PRISMA-guided systematic review of 24 peer-reviewed articles, mapping theoretical, contextual, methodological, and characteristic dimensions of sustainable NPO performance using the TCCM framework.
